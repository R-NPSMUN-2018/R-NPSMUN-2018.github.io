DISEC

“Disarmament, with mutual honor and confidence, is a continuing imperative.”

- Dwight D. Eisenhower

R-npsmun welcomes young visionaries, in the hope of administering a series of intellectually exhilarating debates and stipulations in order to reach a plausible and profitable consensus. The Disarmament and International Security Committee brings together all member states of the UN and thus, is capable of discussing a wide variety of pivotal issues on a broader spectrum. This feature, inevitably, makes DISEC one of the most dynamic committees. According to Article 26 of the United Nations Charter, the DISEC mandate is “to promote the establishment and maintenance of international peace and security with the least diversion for armaments of the world’s human and economic resources.” The Assembly’s goal is to scout for solutions to the challenges in the international security regime, and do so in the hope of resolving hostility and maintaining peace.
